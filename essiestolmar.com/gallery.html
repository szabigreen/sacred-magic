Magnolia digital painting

2015

This was my first ever 100% digital painting. Magnolia was created with Photoshop, within 3-4 days if I remember correctly.

With this artwork I wanted to capture the ethereal beauty of the magnolia trees. When I was a child I hated going to school, but every morning on my way to class I was fascinated by a big magnolia tree on our street, that made my soul smile. Just seeing it blossoms helped me forget about my worries and settle into the present moment.

Nature is such an efficient remedy- no wonder why Buddha got enlightened under a tree... :)

The Magnolia painting can help you if...

  • You want to re-discover the miracles of Nature
  • You thinking way too much
  • You want to step out of your mind

Priestess of the Northern Star

2005

I painted this picture back in 2015 and named her “Priestess of the Northern Star”. Even though it’s an older painting, it’s still one of my favourites.

During the last couple of years I’ve noticed something strange: whenever I looked at her another name popped into my mind: “Karmic Princess”. Back then, I had no idea what the connection was between her and the karma but whenever I took a glimpse at her I felt at ease.

At that time when I was painting it I was in the middle of letting go a lot of my personal karma. People turned up from past lives and turned my life into chaos. So to calm down the turmoil I was intuitively creating “Karmic Princess” to soothe my energies.

I invited in the energies of higher consciousness and painted this picture to help myself remember my true self and to help me return to Awareness. And also to be your reminder throughout your daily challenges that...

Crimson AF

2018

Crimson AF was inspired by Adamus Saint-Germain’s Jan. 2018 Shoud .

There are times, when people are so fallen in love with their daily dramas that even though the solution is lying right in front of their feet, they won’t recognize it.

There are times when people are so invested in their sufferings, that no matter what they won’t let their problems go.

Acting as if you have been controlled by outside forces, playing the victim is a very powerful game. Though the only one who can’t see a helyzet játékos, vicces voltát is the “victim” itself.

So if you have any situation in your life when you encounter suffering, be it a bad relationship, a grave illness or simply just a passing bad mood, I would like you to remember that you are not the victim of your life.

You’ve created this situation, and at some level you must enjoy this drama, otherwise it won’t exist in your life. And the more you oppose, saying that “ I totally don’t enjoy my situation” , the more it is true that you are actually enjoying it. Take some deep breaths and examine the roots of your own problems. You will be amazed by the answers you will find.

But good news:

You meant to be the creator not the victim of your life. So when you are really, 100% ready to stop basking in the sunlight of your “victimhood”, you can let the drama go in an instant.

An alchemist

2014

Back in 2014 I wasn’t consciously aware neither and Adamus Saint-Germain nor the Crimson Circle, but I clearly feel that on an unconscious level this painting was inspired by and Uncle A.

A graphical studio had turned me down, because my art style wasn’t enough “fantasy” for them. So I got frustrated and wanted to show them, that they are wrong…The idea was simple, I decided to draw a fantasy-style warrior in my art style.

Everything went smoothly but in the middle of the painting process, something unusual happened. Even though my fantasy warrior looked perfectly fine from the art technical point of view, I felt a huge sadness and dissatisfaction. I couldn’t stop thinking about that why should I waste this beautiful painting for someone else’s idea, when I clearly wanted to paint an alchemist on this picture. Not a fantasy warrior.

So I covered all the background with a thick layer of acrylic gold paint in order to hide all the fantasy weapons I attached on the back of my warrior, and instead of following any concrete idea, I let myself sinking into the flow …

And as I let go of the battle with myself and stopped controlling the outcome of the painting process, the energies started to transform:

"The warrior had enough fights, so he put down all of his weapons and became a true Alchemist."

The Alchemist can help you in:
  • Facing your light and your darkness
  • Let go of your fights
  • Connecting with your Master-self
  • Understanding the alchemy of the Soul

Lucifer

2015

An unfinished sketch of Lucifer, the lightbringer.

To be honest I’m not really fond of those medieval ages style depiction of her. It always puzzled me how the heck could god’s most beloved angel ended up as an ugly goat-dude on the christian manuscripts.

Well, oookay, of course I know the biblical story how all those stuff happened, but still I find it so funny that humanity is just sooooo afraid of their own darkness, that they projected their fears into a reptile-goat-bat-whatever looking creature… and paid loads of money to the churches just to save them from their own fears.

So I broke up with the tradition and drew her in her original glory.

Why do you have multiple artist names?

In the past I couldn’t make up my mind about the artist names I was going to use, so I had tried all of them out to see which one resonates with me the most. You can find my previous artworks online here and there under the names of Aya Kashi, Extee, Cassandra or Essie/ Eliza Stolmar.

Where do you find inspiration?

That’s a difficult question to answer… while I’m daydreaming a number of ideas, shapes and colors pop into my mind. Sometimes the inspiration and the meaning of the painting only reveal themselves after I’ve finished the art piece. While I’m creating I’m mostly connecting with the wisdom of my Soul but occasionally I have small chats with other non-physical beings as well.
